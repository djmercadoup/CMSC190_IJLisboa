About Impact-Map

Impact map analyzes bands received from the 1990 to 2023 Global Human Modification dataset. This web-app aims to assist researchers in collecting information and informing the general public about trends on anthropogenic stress

About The Global Human Modification dataset

The degree of human modification measures the spatial extent and intensity of land modified by humans. Version 1.5, updated July 2023 based on methods from Theobald et al. 2020. It has been used to measure land modification and create warnings on the increasing rate of land being destroyed and modified by humans

Why use this dataset?

The GHM (Degree of Human Modification) dataset offers a comprehensive view of human impacts on the environment across six major stressor groups, covering the period from 1990 to 2015 at a 300 m resolution compared to the previous 1000m resolution. It includes linear interpolation for missing data years, aligns accurately with water bodies using the ESA 150 m water-mask dataset, and utilizes an updated Global Human Settlement Layer for built-up stressor analysis. With values in 32-bit floating point format ranging from 0.0 to 1.0, the GHM dataset provides a reliable and standardized basis for analyzing long-term environmental trends, land use changes, and urbanization impacts.

gHM takes into account multiple anthropogenic stressors

gHM represents the built-up, crop/pasturelands, grazing, oil & gas production, mining & quarrying, power generation, roads, railways, powerlines, electrical infrastructure, logging, human intrusions, reservoirs, and air pollution stressors, allowing researchers to visualize and measure anthropogenic stress.

Why shouldn't you use the 2017 dataset in comparing gHM values?

The 2017 dataset now includes grazing, oil pollution, and powerlines as stressors, making it have a more holistic view on anthropogenic stress. It has a non-comparable and elevated gHM value compared to 1990-2015 datasets. Due to this, it is not advised to use the 2017 dataset in comparing gHM values from year 1990 - 2015.
